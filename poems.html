  • Ars Poetica (orbitoclast)

    One sterilized orbitoclast
                 pricking the top of your socket—

                 you could feel the winged tine shivering.

    You were panicked. Infracted.

    On the frigid table of the surgery room,
                 begging to slip it out—
                              and the white voice chanting,
                                           now? now?

  • New Snow

    Being from this place, given one or two each year,
    I’d forgotten the appeal of first snow, until
    this morning

    when we stepped out together—you into your first first—
    and I saw you seeing snow—
    saw something in you

    uncovering; until, staring off, you asked if I’d noticed
    that the clearing I loved, visible now
    between the naked trees, was actually

    on a tiny hill—the snow made it obvious—
    and I had to admit, watching you, that no
    I’d never noticed before now.

  • [untitled]

    Tell me again how beautiful, the black zag
                 of leg folded and pulled toward dome
    of chest, set against green as if the hill itself
                 were carved out. As if lack
    lived a life of motion. This is your gift, little one: form,
                 which you brought with you and give
    freely, whether it is wanted or not. My hand was a mare,
                 galloping, once, over a field of wheat, which
    was filled with darkness, which was your father’s
                 back. The tremble of my neck
    was starlight. It was a rippling
                 in the black. Each hoof pounding the earth
    was a spark of dirt, little one.

  • blue, boy

    blue room, blue
    marbles nuzzled planet
    to planet in ziplock bag

    below bed. above, boy
    with blue hands, hands
    jammed full of collisions. boy
    dreams exploded blue
    marbles: stars

    like postponed fireworks, leaky
    sentence ends making
    grottos of blue

    in pale faces. downstairs, blue
    marble adrift in big room. moon
    makes sharp cuts. pearls
    pour over dresser drawer,
    string of iridescent bones
    drawn to surgical light. boy
    trembles. blue

    marble nestles in carpet’s
    fibers before nightstand’s
    open double doors. moon
    cuts a bone slat tooth
    in nightstand’s mouth. journal
    gestures inside nightstand’s
    open cavity,

    full of blue
    ink, full of boy’s
    name, written in mother’s sinuous blue
    script, with marble’s radiant blue
    blood, repeated again and again and blue:

    boy,
    again, & blue,
    boy,
    again, blue,
    boy
